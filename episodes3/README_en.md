## Part 3 Concept

Part 1 began with the search for a single climate solution.

Part 2 let go of that framing and reinterpreted the Trinity Model as auxiliary wheels:

```text
Let the sea breathe.
Cool the air and surface.
Return life to the soil.
```

Part 3 shifts from local practice to social implementation:

- ocean-side auxiliary wheels: where deep-sea aeration could realistically begin;
- air and urban auxiliary wheels: where mist cooling and airflow corridors can become infrastructure;
- soil and watershed auxiliary wheels: where soil restoration, flood control, and ocean load reduction can connect;
- governance, economics, culture, and regional differences;
- the boundary between what can be done now and what must be passed to future generations.
