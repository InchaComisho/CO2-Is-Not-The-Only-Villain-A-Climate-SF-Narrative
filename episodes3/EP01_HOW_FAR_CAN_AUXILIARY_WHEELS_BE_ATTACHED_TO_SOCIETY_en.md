# Part 3 Episode 1 — How far can auxiliary wheels be attached to society?

## Foreword

In Part 2, we let go of the habit of searching for “the only climate solution,” and reinterpreted the Trinity — sea, air, and soil — as auxiliary wheels.

In Part 3, we will follow the question of how far those auxiliary wheels can be attached to society.

Beyond one local community purchase, beyond a community-center-scale experiment, how far can they enter actual sea areas, cities, watersheds, and institutions?

Episode 1 begins by having the protagonist unfold the current map of global climate measures once again.

## Afterword

Thank you for reading.

In Part 3 Episode 1, we unfolded the map for reexamining the Trinity auxiliary wheels from the side of society rather than only from the technical or human side.

The role of this episode is to create an overview for Part 3 as a whole.

On the ocean side, it opens the question of which sea areas could realistically become the first sites for deep-sea aeration.

On the air and urban side, it opens the question of which cities and which urban areas could integrate mist cooling and airflow corridors as infrastructure.

On the soil and watershed side, it opens the question of which rivers and towns can connect soil regeneration and watershed flood control with the burden placed on the sea.

By the end of Part 2, it had become clear that the Trinity is not a miracle technology but auxiliary wheels, that the loss of self-circulating functions in forests, soil, sea, and air lies behind a climate crisis that cannot be explained by emissions alone, and that realistic small repairs can move within the lives of farmers, municipalities, companies, and citizens.

Part 3 now asks how far those auxiliary wheels, which have enough reason to exist, can be expanded into the range where they can actually be implemented.

How do technology, politics, economics, culture, and regional differences interact across sea, air, and soil?

How does the protagonist, as a citizen of one region, connect movements in the wider world to his own life?

The story will follow that again in narrative form.

Episode 1 confirms the starting point: abandon once more the fantasy that auxiliary wheels can be attached everywhere, and begin from places they can reach.

In Episode 2, we will first enter the ocean side more concretely.

The protagonist and the AI will look at what real-world lines would have to be crossed if one were to attach auxiliary wheels to a specific sea.
