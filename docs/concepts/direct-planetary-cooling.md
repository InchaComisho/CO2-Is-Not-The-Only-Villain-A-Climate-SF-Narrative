# Direct Planetary Cooling

## Short Definition

Direct Planetary Cooling is the overarching conceptual framework in the story. It refers to restoring Earth's natural cooling capacity and carbon-fixation circulation — not controlling the planet like a machine, but helping the damaged systems that once managed heat and carbon to function again.

## Role in the Narrative

Direct Planetary Cooling is the framework that unifies all the interventions in the story. From the earliest episodes, the AI character challenges the protagonist's CO₂-only framing by pointing to carbon fixation systems, ocean heat, and soil degradation. By Episode 11, the protagonist arrives at the Trinity Model — the realization that Direct Planetary Cooling requires three simultaneous layers. Episode 12 returns to the Super El Niño map as the final stress test of this framework.

## Conceptual Meaning

Inside Master / InchaComisho's framework, Direct Planetary Cooling means:

- **Deep ocean aeration / ocean circulation restart** — helping the ocean breathe and resume vertical circulation
- **Mist cooling / evaporative cooling** — reducing excessive heat input at the surface
- **Soil regeneration / leaf mold / microorganisms** — restoring terrestrial carbon sinks through biological activity
- **Ecosystem regeneration** — recovering the living systems that absorb, store, and stabilize carbon
- **Restoring living circulation systems** — treating the planet as a living system, not a machine

The concept is explicitly not about geoengineering domination of the climate. It frames intervention as assistance to systems that already exist but have been weakened.
