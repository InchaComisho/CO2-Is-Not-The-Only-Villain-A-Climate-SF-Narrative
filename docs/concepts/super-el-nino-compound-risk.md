# Super El Niño Compound Risk

## Short Definition

Super El Niño Compound Risk is the narrative concept that a very strong El Niño event is not simply a weather anomaly or an unlucky year. It is a stress test layered on an Earth system already weakened by accumulated heat, weakened circulation, degraded ecosystems, and reduced carbon fixation capacity. When all these conditions are present simultaneously, the compound risk is qualitatively different from any individual component.

## Conceptual Meaning

Inside Master / InchaComisho's framework, Super El Niño Compound Risk involves several converging stresses:

- **Baseline warming** — the ocean has more heat stored than in previous El Niño cycles
- **Weakened circulation** — thermohaline circulation and regional upwelling systems are less robust, so normal recovery after El Niño is slower
- **Degraded marine ecosystems** — plankton, coral, and fish populations are already under thermal stress; a Super El Niño pushes them past thresholds
- **Reduced carbon fixation** — both ocean and land carbon sinks are less effective during and after El Niño conditions
- **Soil and food system stress** — drought-flood alternation disrupts agriculture and terrestrial carbon storage simultaneously
- **Cascading ecosystem effects** — disruptions in one system amplify disruptions in adjacent systems

The compound effect makes "Super El Niño" more than the sum of its parts.
