# Ocean Breathing System

## Short Definition

Ocean Breathing System, abbreviated OBS, is the story's implementation name for ocean circulation support through deep aeration. It is described in Episode 5 as a kind of ocean CPR: not replacing the ocean's own circulation, but helping it breathe again after it has been weakened by warming.

## Role in the Narrative

OBS is the technical core of Blue Pulse. It appears across Episodes 5–10 and is the first pillar of the Trinity Model in Episode 11.

- **Episode 5** — introduced: the AI describes the ocean as suffocating from stratification and heat; OBS is the conceptual answer
- **Episode 6** — the Blue Pulse proposal is built around OBS + UMC; it hits the governance wall
- **Episode 7** — the first OBS unit starts operating; the ocean gives ambiguous, slow feedback
- **Episode 8** — decision-makers debate whether to continue Phase 2
- **Episode 9** — OBS deployed in combination with artificial upwelling and UMC off Chile
- **Episode 10** — OBS results weighed: what counts as success; what counts as risk

## Conceptual Meaning

OBS in the narrative represents a specific implementation design within the Deep Ocean Aeration concept:

- nanobubble columns create fine, persistent bubbles that rise slowly through the water column
- the rising column assists vertical mixing without requiring large mechanical energy
- surface mist (UMC) complements OBS by reducing heat input from above

The narrative frames OBS as treating ocean stratification — the physical separation of warm surface water from cold deep water — as the key obstacle to circulation recovery.

## Similar or Adjacent Real-World Ideas

Similar or adjacent ideas include:

- artificial upwelling technology
- bubble plume mixing
- ocean oxygenation
- marine carbon sink restoration
- stratification reduction concepts

These are not identical to the OBS concept in this narrative. OBS is a fictional implementation name that combines several real-world research directions into a single narrative concept.

## Cautions

- OBS is a fictional system name. No operational system under this name exists.
- The narrative does not claim OBS is proven to work at ocean scale.
- Related repositories are conceptual models, not peer-reviewed engineering designs.
