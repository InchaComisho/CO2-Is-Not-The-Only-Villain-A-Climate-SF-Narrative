# Blue Pulse

## Short Definition

Blue Pulse is the fictional project name in the story for the ocean-side climate intervention. It combines two systems:

- **OBS** (Ocean Breathing System) — mixes from below through deep ocean aeration
- **UMC** (Ultrasonic Mist Cooling) — reduces heat input from above through evaporative cooling

Together, they represent an attempt to address ocean heat accumulation and weakened marine circulation from two directions simultaneously.

## Role in the Narrative

Blue Pulse appears mainly in Episodes 6–10. It evolves across these episodes:

- **Episode 6** — the proposal hits its first wall: governance, ethics, who decides, who pays
- **Episode 7** — the first OBS unit starts; the ocean gives ambiguous feedback
- **Episode 8** — the decision meeting is far from the sea; politics and risk tolerance collide
- **Episode 9** — Phase 2 off Chile: artificial upwelling + OBS + UMC field deployment in El Niño-affected waters
- **Episode 10** — evaluation: the protagonist must weigh the risk of action against the risk of doing nothing

Blue Pulse is not presented as a proven solution. It is presented as a speculative but earnest attempt — and the story is as much about the human difficulty of deciding as it is about the technology.

## Conceptual Meaning

Inside Master / InchaComisho's framework, Blue Pulse is a narrative synthesis of:

- deep ocean aeration (OBS)
- evaporative surface cooling (UMC)
- ocean tuning and monitoring infrastructure (OTU)

It represents the idea that ocean heat cannot be removed by CO₂ reduction alone, and that an active intervention — however uncertain — may be necessary to support the ocean's own circulation recovery.
