# Part 2 Episode 11 — Even so, try running the flow once

## Foreword

Episode 10 created a loose local framework for joint purchasing.

Episode 11 shows the first run.

Small successes, small failures, logistics, timing, quantity, trust, and the reason to continue all become visible once the flow actually moves.

---

“Since it is the first time, expect some accidents.”

The organizer said that in the community center room.

I could only answer, “Of course.”

Same tatami.

Same long tables.

Same tea pot.

But today was different.

Last time was a discussion.

Today was delivery.

The first run of the loose joint purchase.

---

## Section 1 — What worked first

The first surprise was that people came.

Not a crowd.

But more than expected.

The café had posted once.

The municipality had introduced it lightly.

The farmer had talked to a few people.

Residents had shared it.

“So one voice from each actor created the first circle.”

‘Yes.’

It was small.

But no longer imaginary.

---

## Section 2 — Too much and not enough

Some people said the vegetable box was larger than expected.

Some still needed to go to the supermarket afterward.

Both were true.

The joint purchase did not replace daily life.

It changed one small part of it.

“That may be why it feels possible.”

‘Because it does not demand everything.’

A partial repair is easier to continue than a total conversion.

---

## Section 3 — Time does not fit everyone

Some people could not come.

Work changed.

Family schedules changed.

The pick-up time was inconvenient.

A friend-pick-up option might be needed.

A wider time window might help.

“Do not call them unmotivated just because the time did not fit.”

‘Exactly.’

A continuing system must absorb ordinary life.

---

## Section 4 — Do not cover everything with righteousness

At the café corner, someone said:

“If everything is described as ‘for the soil’ or ‘for the environment,’ people may stiffen.”

The café owner nodded.

“Good food comes first. Then people can learn that the taste is connected to soil.”

That changed me.

Maybe the entrance should be food, life, and local enjoyment.

Soil, sea, and future can sit behind them, quietly supporting the meaning.

---

## Section 5 — No perfect result, but no reason to stop

At the end, the organizer listed the problems.

Quantity.

Time.

Pick-up method.

Communication.

But then he smiled.

“There is not much reason to stop.”

That phrase felt more real than “we have many reasons to continue.”

A little easier for people.

A little easier for soil.

A little easier for the sea.

That may be enough to run it again.

---

## Section 6 — Toward the conclusion of Part 2

At home, I looked back from Episode 1 to Episode 11.

CO₂ graphs.

Population.

Forests.

Soil.

Sea.

Farmers.

Municipalities.

Companies.

Citizens.

Shopping baskets.

Joint purchasing.

Part 2 had not found a perfect solution.

It had found a way to move something better once.

And that was the bridge to the final episode.

---

## Afterword

Thank you for reading.

Episode 11 shows the first run of the loose joint purchase. It is not perfect. Quantity, timing, pick-up, and communication all need improvement.

But the key feeling is that there is not much reason to stop. A small system that can continue is more valuable than a perfect system that collapses immediately.

This episode prepares the conclusion of Part 2 by showing that “something better” can actually move once.
